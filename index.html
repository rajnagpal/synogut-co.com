Black Walnut Hull is known for its antiparasitic properties. It has been traditionally used to expel intestinal parasites and worms, ensuring a healthier gut environment.

Aloe Vera

Aloe Vera is not just beneficial for skin but also for the digestive system. It has natural soothing properties that can help reduce inflammation in the gut and promote healing of the digestive tract.

Lactobacillus Acidophilus

Lactobacillus Acidophilus is a probiotic strain that plays a crucial role in maintaining a balanced gut microbiome. It helps in promoting the growth of beneficial bacteria while keeping harmful bacteria in check.

Glucomannan Root

Glucomannan Root is a type of dietary fiber derived from the konjac plant. It aids in digestion by promoting a feeling of fullness, reducing appetite, and supporting regular bowel movements.
7

Flaxseed

Flaxseed is a rich source of dietary fiber and omega-3 fatty acids. It aids in digestion by promoting regularity and also supports heart health due to its omega-3 content.
8

Apple Pectin

Apple Pectin is a type of soluble fiber found in apples. It aids in digestion by promoting regular bowel movements and also helps in maintaining healthy blood sugar levels.
9

Oat Bran

Oat Bran is rich in both soluble and insoluble fiber. It aids in digestion by promoting bowel regularity and also helps in reducing cholesterol levels in the body.

Prune Extract

Prune Extract is a natural laxative known for its effectiveness in promoting regular bowel movements. It helps in softening stools and is often recommended for individuals suffering from constipation.
